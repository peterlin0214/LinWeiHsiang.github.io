ZHEN PIN NETWORK is a company dedicating in developing mobile game with new business model, which transfers physical card and chess games to online platform, and more than that, by "player room" mechanism to achieve viral marketing.

  • Revenue model and backstage design

      Challenges

      • - No Experience in Mahjong
            I had to help design backstage of mahjong game while I hadn't played it before.
      • - Uncertain Revenue Model
            I needed to figure out how to attract and gain dealers with easy-understanding model.
    • Revenue model design

      •     Our business model was using "Player Room" mechanism to achieve viral marketing among mahjong players, and selling "Room Card" to create revenue. Main competitors, such as "神來也" and “明星三缺一”, were featuring quick match game. However, there were players seeking more "realistic" playing experience and preferring to play with friends instead of computer players.
          With some market surveys, those highly addicted players were 40~50 and had strong networks among their friends, who also played mahjong. Thus, I targeted those players and tried to design a revenue model that would fit our business model.
          There were two primary designs, multilevel direct selling and pyramid structure.


      1. Multilevel direct selling
            This mechanism is familiar to many people in Taiwan. The bonus sound attractive. However, there were problems along with its benefits.
        • Agents might stockpile game cards and cause unstable cash flow.
        • Direct selling had had bad reputation and been associated with fraud.
        • Backstage would be more complicated due to different bonus calculation between multiple-level structure.
      2. Dealer pyramid
            This structure was better fitted to our business model. We generally divided our structure into three layers. The bottom were agents, who bought room cards from dealers or company, if there were no corresponding dealers. The middle layer were dealers, who took greater discount from company and sold game cards to agents.
            To make this easier to understand, we simply used "gold dealer" and "silver dealer" to represent agent and dealer mentioned above.By this structure, there were some benefits.
        • Dealers of each stage could focus on their roles.
               Silver dealers have more margin profits; however, they have to deal with customer issues and act like CSRs. Because players(end-users) are their income sources, they would focus on how to attract more players or stimulate more consumption.
               On the other hand, gold dealers' profits come from play cards sold to silver dealers. Thus, they would focus on how to promote more players to become silver dealers. Because gold dealers got to be silver dealers at the first place, they know what kind of problems silver dealers could encounter and act like mentors.
        • Dealers can chose their position
              Dealers have to meet certain requirments to go to upper stage. However, we concern that some dealers may prefer and suit particular stage than others. Thus, we allow dealers that is qualified to stay at their original stage. This mechanism help every dealer to perform better. For example, one could be qualified to move from silver dealer to golden, which spends much less time and effort to deal with players. However, if the dealer is passionate about developing new players, he could chose not to become golden dealer.
        • Backstage would be more simple.
              We didn't have mcuh time to modify the backstage structure. By adopting pyramid structure, our backstage would be simple for dealer. They can view how many cards are in stock, how many cards have been sold to whom within specific duration. Dealers just need to buy cards from upper dealer and control their stocks. Their profit is easy to calculate. This is a benefit for not only dealers but also the company.
        • Avoild stocking
              In traditional direct selling structure, since the bonus is given once the product is sold by downlines, some would even force thier downlines to stock products, or even create downlines that are actually themselves. By contrast, because dealers' profit is gain by margin between cost and revenue, there would be no way to create additional profit by stocking products. This also helps us to have a better control of selling operation.

    • Backstage Design

      •     After surveying our target audience, we realized must of them were at the age between 40~45. Taking the user experience into consideraton, I designed the backstage buttons to provide necessary functions as less confussion as possible. The original backstage had 27 buttons for dealers. I managed to reduce to 18. It was not just downsizing the framework but also being aware of what users, including administrator and dealers, might need. It took many steps to come up with final solution.

      1. What business goals do stakeholders concern?
            Just like data science, in which the first step is to understand the purpose of collecting data, the fisrt step of designing what buttons were needed was to list crucial business goals with priority.

      2. What methods are used to help gaining insights and decision making?
            After listing the business goals being concerned, the next step was figuring out what kinds of methods could help stakeholders with decision making to achieve certain business goals.

      3. What data are required
            The last step before designing buttons was to think over what kinds of data were required for those methods.

          4. Take one example: For golden dealers

        • Find out the main customers(silver dealer)
          > Accumulated purchase amount of silver dealers within specific duration
          > List sorted by number of cards purchased by silver dealers with duration selection
          > Button【 Selling Detail 】
          • Selector: time duration
          • Output: silver dealer's ID, number of cards purchased, consumption percentage, number of cards consumed
          • Filter: number of cards purchased, consumption percentage
        • Solve the stocking issue for silver dealers if there is one.
          > The consumption amount compared to purchase amount
          > List sorted by consumption percentage within specific duration.
          > Button【 Selling Detail 】

          •     As the【 Selling Detail 】button above, it came through a series of process. At first, there were two buttons related to each purpose; however, we found out the table could be combined. There were other buttons for checking the general selling situation or with specified silver dealer.

It's a great honor having the opportunity to run a company with incredible partners. INQTECH was founded in 2016 with friends back to college. That was a long story. Briefly put, we dedicate to apply AI technology to AOI(Automated Optical Inspection) industry.

  • Finance
    • Budgeting

      •     Developing annual budgets without previos finance data meant we had to come to consensus of annual business plan and estimate related operating activities in collaboration with the development department or other parties included in annual plan. For example, business development department might have planned to join five exhibitions. Only if we had a clear roadmap of what we were going to do and how we would going to make it, we could estimate revenue and cost based on those activities.

      1. Revenue and cost estimation

            This came with three parts; fist, the revenue estimation. This relatively denpended on annual activities more directly than cost and expense estimation. However, this might be the hardest part because it was hard to precisely tell the revenue every activity or project could bring. Thus, we came up with a range of prediction under different circumstances.
            Second, the interaction of cost and revenue, which mainly focused on direct costs coming along with revenue, such as direct materials and direct wages expense.
            Third, other operating expense. This included two parts, the indirect costs related to revenue generating activities and normal operating expense. The normal operating expense was nearly fixed, and the indirect costs were estimated to be ratio of direct costs.

            At this stage, we had different annual finance predictions with different scenarios. We selected one according to executability, desirability and variation possibituy as our annual budget base.


      2. Risk control

            One of the most fatal issue in not only corporate finance, but also individual financial decision is that rasing budget or investment even when the performance is not as well as expected, "The Gambler's Fallacy" so called. To avoid getting into that problem, we carefully gave each project a range of budget increment based on the performance.
            For example, we planned to develop measurement module with 400 labor hours and ready to be deployed on our AOI system, and we thought this development project was important so we gave a 20% tolerance. If we had spent 200 hours with 45% job down, this would be acceptable to raise labor hour budget within the boundary we set.
            However, if the project was way beyond our predetermination, we had to shut it down. Of course that meant wasting money and resources with nothing in return. Thus, a well conducted accounting operation was required to help us spot any potential risk from scratch.


      3. Budget Adjustment and Identifying Opportunities

            Another part of budgeting is making adjustment after a period. I reviewed our spending based on performance and compared them to budget. Sometimes, it was simply underperformed. However, if the spending of similar activities or projects were all over budget, we had to make adjustment toward next period's budgeting.
            For example, the utilities expense was correlated with labor hours with certain ratio, which meant using labor hour as allocation bases was not a problem. However, if the utilities expenses were all much higher than budgets under different labor hours, we must recalculate the indirect cost rate to adjust our budgeting. On the other hand, if there were expenses less than budgets without affecting operating performance, it might indicate there was excess capacity.

    • Accounting

      •      Technical know-how, finance, team and management, marketing may be the most crucial elements for a startup. Finanacial management and budgeting play an important role, especially when the cash flow is unstable and business model still needs to be tested by market and adjusted.


      1. Book keeping

            Book keeping sounds to be the easiest part in accounting area, that's why it is often neglected within startups. However, this is a critical part. Without a comprehensive book keeping, it will be hard to do the next step, like analyzing financial situation, making sensitive analysis or next period's budgeting.
            My job was to sitck to management accounting to make sure all financial activities were properly recorded. The biggest challenge was how to recognize indirect costs and how to distribute costs to different kind of activities. This was so important because we often had multiple projects going on at the same time. Knowing how many time and resources were distributed to each project would help track and evaluate perofrmance, and adjust project budget.


      2. Analysis

            This was a disturbing problem because it was hard to use other companies' financial situation to evaluate our performance. Although we could found financial report of listed company, it might not be the appropriate standard for us. What we did could be divided into few parts.

        •   (1) Major activity

              We focused on major business activites that directly influenced revenue and expense. Because our revenue was mainly generated through diversified projects. We consistantly analyzed our projects' performance and other activites related to project execution.
              For example, we found the conversion rate from exhibitions was better than expected. Although the direct and indirect cost related to joining exhibitions were also more expensive than estimated, the revenue generated from exhibitions was still higher than the expense. Thus, joinging exhibitions might be a good idea for revenue growth.

        •   (2) Cost structure and annual analysis

              With comprehensive records, we could figure out the correlation between each activity. For example, the material, supplies and wages expense regarding R&D would be higher during the month when we had exhibition activity. To be more precise, the extra expense depended on the number of cases under evaluation.
              With this knowledge, we could calculate the ratio of direct cost and indirect cost correlated to their dependency activities or allocation bases.
              This helped us to measure whether there was any unuasual performance and establish more accurate budgets. Take previos example, if the wages expense was higher than monthly average in the month without exhibition, we could check if the development department actually had more cases under evaluation. If the answer was no, that meant something had gone wrong.
              We could measure the annual performance. By investigating montlhy income statement through the year and comparing actual expense to budgets, we could find some issues, like the unusual wages expense mentioned before. However, if the expense of particular category was always less or more than predetermined budget. It could mean we had underestimated the expense, or on the other hand, there were excess capacities. This helped us to adjust next period's budgets and make better decisions.

        •   (3) Critical index

              This part was mainly focused on annual financial estimation and important index, such as current ratio.
              With the data of previous period and analysis results mentioned above, we could arrange our annual financial estimation by using income statement, financial statement and cash flow statement and check some important index to see if we might have cash shortage or other problem.

      3. Alternative plans

            Planning annual budget and risk control was just basic guideline for the upcoming operation. However, what if the performance was just not good enough to generate necessary cash flow? Should we increase more spending on advertisement and other exposure events, or cut it down? When we planned our annual budget, we had a process kind like decision tree. Thus, it could be our potential plan B, C or more. When the performance was severely detached from our expectation, we would consider changing our original plan
            Still, all decisions should be made with sufficient analysis. Most of the time we would be more cautious with spending. If the opportunity was not obvious and the profit couldn't be guaranteed, it would be better to stick to budget limitation.
